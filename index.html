The Honest Part

What smart people say back.

Sequence-of-returns risk
A crash near retirement is disproportionately costly. The paper's counterargument: international diversification hedges this better than bonds historically have.
The US may have been exceptional
Cederburg uses global data precisely because US-only returns may overstate expectations. Survivor bias in any historical dataset is real.
Behavioral risk
The optimal portfolio on paper is not optimal if you sell in a 50% drawdown. Most people cannot hold through one. That is the actual variable.
Early retirement access
"Never touch it" means don't panic sell — not that you can't access the money. Roth contributions (not earnings) are withdrawable anytime. A taxable brokerage bridges from early retirement to 59½. The Roth conversion ladder extends that further for anyone who plans five years ahead.
